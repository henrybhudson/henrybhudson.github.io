Medical Imaging
How computing has improved medical imaging.
MRI Scans
Computing has been able to improve MRI scans by speeding up the imaging process, resulting in shorter scan times therefore image quality as it gives the patient less time to move around. This movement (called a motion artifact) would disrupt the imaging and decrease quality of the images. It has also led to enhanced image quality through noise reduction and artifact correction.

Computing enables 3D imaging, which provides a much more comprehensive view of anatomical information. It also facilitates advanced analysis of data, allowing for more precise measurements and monitoring of any disease progression and treatment effectiveness quickly.
Sources: Magn Reson Med Sci, News Medical
PET Scanning
Computers help PET scans to investigate the effectiveness of various treatments as well as to evaluate the health of a person's body. It achieves this by enabling:
  • Image Recontruction - calculating the location of the source within the patient from the signals received by cameras.
  • Image Processing - noise reduction of signals and artifact removal.
  • Analysis & AI - PET data can be fed through complex modelling and AI can be used to assist in pattern recognition and disease detection.
  • Data Sharing & Storage - computing supports the management, retrieval and sharing of PET data.
It is clear that the entire process of PET scanning would be impossible without computers.
Sources: EJNMMI, Johns Hopkins
